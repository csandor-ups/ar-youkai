AR Yōkai

A Situated Augmented Reality Ghost Story

Motivation:

Currently AR content is placed and enjoyed anywhere, regardless of location or environment context. However, AR should be intertwined to the context, and environment to maximize immersiveness. AR Yōkai is a project to visualize an immersive AR user experience with storytelling and design based on the surrounding environment. We showcase this experience with the Microsoft Hololens.

Making of:

The characters showcased in AR Yōkai are derived from ancient Japanese Paintings. These paintings depict the ghosts from ancient folklore, and are typically tied closely with older buildings. We render and animate the 3D models of the characters using the paintings as a reference, then develop the story based option the ancedotal ties of each Yōkai in its given environment.

Contribution:

AR Yōkai was created with the aim of clarifying whether the experience of the player improves when there is a strong connection between the AR content and the surrounding environment. This is contrary to current trends which do not consider the surrounding environment. However, we think that this is fundamental to an immersive AR experience. We aim to further improve the AR experiences through characters which are tied to the location and environment.
